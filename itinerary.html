Itinerary

Seceda and the Odle · photo: Wolfgang Moroder / CC BY-SA 3.0

The recommended plan is "mountains to sea": Dolomites hiking while the Alps are at their best, the lead course in Arco in week 2, and, if we extend, Kalymnos exactly as it comes good. The 3-week core stands alone; weeks 4 and 5 clip on without reshuffling anything.

Why this order and not the reverse

Early September is the Dolomites' best fortnight and the huts and lifts start closing from about the 20th, so hiking goes first. Arco is prime all month, so the course sits comfortably in the middle, after our legs are fit and our heads are used to exposure from the via ferrata. Kalymnos only gets better as October approaches, so it takes the tail. The same dates reversed would have us on a hot island in week 1 and in a closing-down Cortina in week 5.

Weeks 1-2: Dolomites

Two bases, because there is no fast public transport between the western and eastern Dolomites. Val Gardena first (best car-free valley in the range: the guest pass makes every bus free), then across to the Cortina side for the eastern icons.

Sat 29 - Sun 30 Aug
Fly Brisbane to Venice. Train to Bolzano and bus 350 up to Ortisei (about 5-6 h door to door), or grab the hire car if we choose one. Sleep like the dead.
Mon 31 Aug
Gentle legs-on day: Alpe di Siusi meadow loops, lunch at Gostner Schwaige (the hay soup place). Buy the guest-pass basics, book any remaining lift cards.
Tue 1 Sept
Seceda ridgeline. Ride up, walk the rim, descend on foot via Col Raiser to dodge the €74 return fare.
Wed 2 Sept
Adolf Munkel trail under the Odle north faces in Val di Funes, lunch at Geisleralm.
Thu 3 Sept
Puez-Odle high traverse, the all-dayer. Bus back on the pass.
Fri 4 Sept
Via ferrata day one: Sass Rigais with a Catores guide, or self-guided Tridentina with rented kits if we are feeling solid. This is the bridge toward the course.
Sat 5 Sept
Transfer to the Cortina side (bus via Val Pusteria, about 3.5 h). Evening stroll, gelato audit.
Sun 6 Sept
Tre Cime di Lavaredo circuit plus the Cadini viewpoint spur if it is open and dry. Pre-booked toll slot or the Dobbiaco bus.
Mon 7 Sept
Lago di Sorapis, boots on the trail by 07:00 to beat the crowd.
Tue 8 Sept
Lagazuoi: up the Kaiserjäger path, down through the WWI tunnels (headlamps), then the Cinque Torri trenches and a long lunch at Rifugio Averau. Arguably the best single day in the Dolomites.
Wed 9 Sept
Croda da Lago circuit to Lago Federa, or a rest day if the legs vote no.
Thu 10 Sept
Travel to Arco: bus to Bolzano or Trento, train south, local bus to Arco (half a day). Afternoon wander down the gear-shop street purely for reconnaissance. No buying yet.

Hut-to-hut variant: swap days 6-9 for a 4-day Alta Via 1 central section (Passo Falzarego to Passo Duran) if the rifugio emails come back positive. Same scenery family as the day-hike version, plus the hut evenings; minus flexibility if weather turns.

Weeks 2-3: Arco

Fri 11 - Sun 13 Sept
Settle in. A lake day at Riva or Torbole, a warm-up top-rope session, easy walks in the olive terraces. If the course is the 4-half-day format, it can start here.
Mon 14 - Wed 16 Sept
The lead course, 3 days with a UIAGM guide: clipping, lead belays, practice falls, threading anchors, bailing. The whole trip funnels through these three days.
Thu 17 Sept
Buy the kit in town with course-fresh knowledge: rope, draws, harnesses, helmets, belay devices. See Gear for the list and budget. Afternoon: first fully independent leads at Massone on our own rope.
Fri 18 - Sat 19 Sept
Consolidation days at Nago and Baone. Easy grades, perfect technique, lots of volume. Celebration dinner in the old town.
Sun 20 Sept
3-week version: train to Venice or Milan, fly home. Extension version: bus to Bergamo for the Kos flight.

Week 4 (option): Kalymnos on our own gear

Sun 20 - Mon 21 Sept
Ryanair Bergamo to Kos (seasonal direct), taxi to Mastichari, 30-45 min ferry to Pothia, taxi to Massouri. Studio for the fortnight, scooter from the rental up the road.
Tue 22 - Sun 27 Sept
The routine that makes people move here: climb 8:00-13:00 on morning-shade sectors (Arhi, Kasteli, Arginonta valley), swim and siesta, taverna dinner, Fatolitis bar. One boat-taxi day to Glaros on Telendos. Optional half-day with Simon Montmory or Climb in Kalymnos to check our systems as new leaders.

4-week version: fly Kos to Athens on ~27-28 Sept and home from there.

Week 5 (option): the festival finish

Mon 28 Sept - Thu 1 Oct
More mileage as the temperatures drop into the sweet spot. Vathy fjord kayak day or the Italian-war-history walk up Profitis Ilias for the legs.
Fri 2 - Sun 4 Oct
Kalymnos International Climbing Festival: clinics, films, excursions, and the whole island turned into one big climbers' evening. The natural crescendo for the trip.
Mon 5 Oct
Ferry, Kos to Athens flight, long haul home from Athens.

Alternative shapes we considered

Plan B: Iberia

Picos de Europa or Ordesa hiking in weeks 1-2, then the verified 5-day "From the Gym to the Rock" course at Siurana (EUR 450 including crag transport), then Margalef or Chulilla as conditions cool. The same correct season shape as Italy. Costs a touch less; needs a car the whole way and a 4-7 hour internal drive; no single town matches Arco's course-plus-shops convenience. Lives on as the fallback if Italy pricing or availability goes wrong.

Plan C: one-country Italy

For maximum adventure-to-transit ratio: Dolomites, Arco course, then finish at Finale Ligure on the Ligurian coast for sea-cliff sport climbing and seafood, all by train and bus, no flights after landing. The 4-week version without leaving Italy. Weaker climbing-social scene than Kalymnos and some polished classic routes, but zero transit friction and superb food throughout. The 5-week version of this plan gets its own festival finale: loop back to Arco for Rock Master, 2-4 October, the sport's most storied competition in its 40th-anniversary edition, then fly home from Venice or Milan.

Plan D: course on Kalymnos instead

Dolomites weeks 1-2, fly to Kos around 12 September, a mid-September "Take the lead" session with Simon Montmory (2026 dates by enquiry), then climb on our own gear as the island cools, with the festival as the finale if we run 5 weeks. One less course-booking unknown since the dates are published, and the course itself is outstanding. The cost: hotter course days (mid-September shade tactics), gear bought on the island (fine, roughly 7 shops, but less choice than Arco), and committing to Greece before we have met the rock. This is the main live alternative to the recommendation; it is on the Decisions page.

Dates that shape the plan

  • Sat 12 Sept: Sellaronda Bike Day closes the Sella passes to cars 9:00-16:00. Fine car-free, a trap if driving that day.
  • ~20 Sept: Dolomites huts and several lifts begin closing; the hiking leg should be done by then.
  • 2-4 Oct: festival weekend, twice over. The Kalymnos climbing festival and Arco's Rock Master (40th edition) run on the same dates. A 5-week trip can end at one or the other, not both. Book rooms early either way.
