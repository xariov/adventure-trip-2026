The lead climbing course

Kalymnos limestone · photo: alpguide / CC BY-SA 2.0

This is the anchor of the whole trip: learn to lead sport routes outdoors, walk out of the course competent and confident, buy our own kit, then climb on it. Everything else on this site is arranged around where and when we do this.

Recommendation: do the course in Arco, Italy, in trip week 2

Arco is the best course-then-buy-gear town found anywhere: September is its prime season, UIAGM guide prices are the lowest of any researched location (EUR 170-340 per person for a stacked basic + lead course, or roughly EUR 700-855 per person for a dedicated 3-day private course), the town has an extraordinary density of climbing shops for kitting up afterwards, and the beginner crags around town are ideal for consolidating first leads. It also sits 2-3 hours from the Dolomites, so hiking and climbing chain together with almost no transit cost.

Strong alternative: Simon Montmory's 5-day "Take the lead" course on Kalymnos, Greece. It is the most complete packaged syllabus found, and group sessions run through September and October (2026 dates are by enquiry; his site does not publish a fixed calendar). The trade-off is that mid-September on Kalymnos is still hot and the island really turns on from late September.

What the course must cover

Whichever provider we pick, this is the checklist. Anything not explicitly listed on a provider's syllabus, we confirm by email before booking.

  • Lead clipping: clipping positions, rope direction, avoiding back-clipping and z-clipping.
  • Lead belaying: feeding, catching falls, soft catches, assisted-braking device use.
  • Fall practice: taking and catching practice falls until they are boring.
  • Cleaning and threading anchors: lowering off, threading fixed rings, re-tying mid-system.
  • Retreat and problem-solving: bailing off a route, stuck rope, communication.
  • Gear knowledge: what to buy, how to inspect it, rope care.

The exact course to book, by name

The naming trap

Provider websites are full of taster sessions, kids' camps and multi-pitch trips, and the beginner lead course is almost never called "beginner lead course". The pattern: anything named basic, beginner or intro is a top-rope product for people who have never climbed; the learn-to-lead syllabus hides under advanced (Arco), Take the lead (Kalymnos) or from the gym to the rock (Spain). We already climb in the gym, so the "beginner" products are the wrong ones for us. This table names the exact product to pick on each site.

Why Arco wins

Season

September and October are rated among the best climbing days of the year at Arco: stable weather, 20-25°C. Our window lands entirely in prime time.

Course value

UIAGM-guided courses from EUR 170. Even the premium private 3-day option costs about the same as the packaged island courses, and dates are on request rather than fixed.

The gear-shop street

Around 17 outdoor shops along 300 m of the old town, including three Oliunìd stores. Finish the course, walk down the street, and buy the full kit with fresh advice. See Gear.

First-lead crags

Massone (the classic first-lead venue, shaded sectors), Baone's friendly slabs, and Nago's close-bolted lower sector with lake views. Days of consolidation climbing within minutes of town.

Rock Master 2026: 2-4 October, and it is the 40th edition

Arco's famous Rock Master competition runs 2-4 October 2026 at the Climbing Stadium (an earlier version of this page had it in early September; the official site says October). So it does not touch our course window at all: early-September Arco is normal shoulder season with no event crunch. It does open an option: it is the same weekend as the Kalymnos climbing festival, so a 5-week trip can end at one festival or the other. If we stay Italy-only, finishing back in Arco for Rock Master's 40th anniversary would be a spectacular free-entry finale; rooms for that weekend need booking well ahead.

The Kalymnos alternative, honestly

Kalymnos is the most famous learn-to-climb island in the world and the "Take the Lead" course is superb on paper. Two honest caveats from the research:

  • Heat: the island's season really starts mid-October. A mid-September course week means chasing shade; late September is transitional and improving.
  • Sequencing: doing the course there in week 3 means hiking first and climbing last, which works, but gear buying happens on the island (about 7 shops in Masouri, so genuinely fine) and any post-course climbing depends on extending to 4-5 weeks.

The strongest version of the Kalymnos option: course in Arco in week 2 anyway, then fly to Kalymnos in week 4 as new leaders when conditions are coming good, with a day of supervised first-leads from a local instructor as a refresher. Both islands' details are on Destinations.

Booking plan

  1. Decide Arco vs Kalymnos for the course (see Decisions).
  2. If Arco: email Arco Mountain Guide and Mmove this month with our dates and the syllabus checklist above; ask for a private-pair quote for 3 days and a group price. Book whichever confirms the full syllabus.
  3. If Kalymnos: message Simon Montmory now (booking form or WhatsApp) for "Take the lead" session dates in our window, then hold the spot with the 30% deposit.
  4. Either way, book course dates before accommodation for that leg, then lock rooms around them.
