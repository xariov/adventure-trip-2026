Itinerary

Seceda and the Odle · photo: Wolfgang Moroder / CC BY-SA 3.0

Five weeks, departing 1 September. The Italian fortnight is planned in detail below: Dolomites first, then the course and the kits in Arco, leaving Italy around the 17th. The last three weeks are open on purpose; what follows for them is a well-researched corridor and the short list of things worth booking even so.

The order isn't negotiable with the calendar. Early September is the Dolomites' best fortnight, Arco is good all month, and everything south of the Alps improves as October approaches, so Italy goes first and the warm places wait. The tail runs south-east because that is where our shortlist lives and because the flight home leaves from Athens.

Weeks 1-2: the Italian fortnight

Two Dolomites bases, because there is no fast public transport between the western and eastern valleys: Val Gardena first (works fully without a car; the guest pass makes every bus free), then either the Cortina side for the eastern peaks or the hut traverse below. To Arco by the 11th.

Tue 1 - Wed 2 Sept
Fly Brisbane to Venice (departures around 21:00-22:00, landing early afternoon on the 2nd). Train to Bolzano and bus 350 up to Ortisei, or pick up a hire car at Venice if the Dolomites get one. Evening arrival, sleep like the dead.
Thu 3 Sept
Gentle legs-on day: Alpe di Siusi meadow loops, lunch at Gostner Schwaige (the hay soup place). Sort the guest pass and any lift cards.
Fri 4 Sept
Seceda ridgeline. Pre-book the lift slot (new for 2026); ride up, walk the rim, come down on foot via Col Raiser to dodge the €74 return fare.
Sat 5 Sept
Adolf Munkel trail under the Odle north faces in Val di Funes, lunch at Geisleralm.
Sun 6 Sept
Puez-Odle high traverse, the all-dayer. Bus back on the pass.
Mon 7 Sept
Via ferrata day: Sass Rigais with a Catores guide, or self-guided Tridentina with rented kits. The bridge toward the course, and the day that decides whether our insurance needs the via ferrata clause used.
Tue 8 Sept
Transfer to the Cortina side (buses via Val Pusteria, about 3.5 h). Evening on Corso Italia, gelato audit.
Wed 9 Sept
Tre Cime di Lavaredo circuit plus the Cadini viewpoint spur if it is open and dry. Pre-booked toll slot or the Dobbiaco bus.
Thu 10 Sept
Lagazuoi: up the Kaiserjäger path, down through the WWI tunnels (headlamps), then the Cinque Torri trenches and a long lunch at Rifugio Averau. Arguably the best single day in the Dolomites.
Fri 11 Sept
Travel to Arco (bus and train via Bolzano or Trento, half a day). First pass of the gear-shop street; if we are buying before the course, the shopping starts here.
Sat 12 - Sun 13 Sept
Kits, a warm-up top-rope session, a lake day at Riva or Torbole. Lago di Sorapis and Croda da Lago wait for another trip unless a spare day appears.
Mon 14 - Wed 16 Sept
The lead course with a UIAGM guide: clipping, lead belays, practice falls, threading anchors, bailing. On our own gear if we bought first. The whole trip funnels through these days.
Wed 16 - Thu 17 Sept
First fully independent leads at Massone and Nago on our own ropes. Celebration dinner at Alla Lega (closed Wednesdays, so aim for Thursday). Then leave Italy for the corridor: about two weeks in, as agreed, earlier if restless, later only if besotted.

The hut traverse, if the emails come back easy

The alternative to the Cortina base for days 7-11: walk the central Alta Via 1 from Passo Falzarego to Passo Duran, the most scenic stretch and the easiest to book. Two workable shapes, both inside our window with every hut open (nothing on this route closes before 20 September; the paced version fits 5-9 September after the Val Gardena days):

  • 4 days, 3 nights: Falzarego → Cinque Torri → Nuvolau → Croda da Lago (night) → Città di Fiume → Coldai (night) → Tissi → Vazzoler (night) → Carestiato → Passo Duran. Days of 4-7 hours.
  • 5 days, 4 nights (better paced): short first day Falzarego → Nuvolau (night), then Croda da Lago (night), Coldai (night), Vazzoler (night), out via Carestiato to Passo Duran. Leaves room for one weather day.

Contacts for the target huts, verified on official hut and CAI pages in July 2026. Half-board where published runs €73-80 (CAI members around €58-63). The Veneto huts take €15-35 per-person deposits, mostly non-refundable; Carestiato just holds a card and cancels free to 20 days out. Vazzoler is cash only, and Coldai, Tissi and Carestiato all warn their card terminals fail, so the Civetta stretch runs on cash.

The three open weeks (~17 Sept - 6 Oct)

Nothing below is booked or fixed; it is the corridor we would most likely follow, laid out so the on-the-road decisions are picks rather than research. Montenegro, the Albanian Alps and Kalymnos lead the shortlist, they chain naturally by bus and ferry, and the whole arc ends where the flight home leaves.

Where it flexes: drop Durmitor and the corridor gains two island days and loses two bus days; drop Albania and Montenegro gets a week with the car; drop both and it is a fortnight of Kalymnos with day trips to Telendos, Vathy and Kos. Nothing before the island needs deciding until we are on the ground in Kotor.

What gets booked despite the looseness: refundable Kalymnos rooms covering 30 September to 5 October before the island fills for the festival; the Tirana-Athens and Athens-Kos flights three to six weeks out, because availability holds but the cheap fares go; the Koman ferry online a day or two ahead (one 09:00 boat, and the same booking chains the Shkodra and Valbona minibuses); Albanian guesthouses by WhatsApp a few days out, with euro cash for everything up there; and the daily Kotor-Shkodra bus (Old Town Travel, around €25) the day before on busticket4.me.

All four were researched to the same depth as the corridor; sources below. The flight home is Athens-based, so any of these would mean re-shaping the exit; that is the main price of switching.

Dates that matter across the five weeks: Qatar's Doha-Venice leg runs only about four days a week, so the outbound needs a day-check before booking Qatar; the Seceda lift and the Tre Cime toll road both want pre-booked slots; the Kalymnos festival is 2-4 October and its rooms are the one scarce thing in the open weeks; and the flight home prices best on Tuesday 6 or Wednesday 7 October.
