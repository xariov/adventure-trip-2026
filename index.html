The plan

Five weeks, departing Brisbane on 1 September. The first two weeks are Italy and they are the planned part: land in Venice on the 2nd, hike the Dolomites while they are at their best (Tre Cime, Seceda, the Lagazuoi war tunnels, a via ferrata or two to get comfortable on steep rock), then drop south to Arco around the 10th for the lead course with a UIAGM guide, a kit each from the town's seventeen gear shops, and the first independent leads above Lake Garda.

The last three weeks are open on purpose. We leave Italy around mid-month with skills, gear and no fixed schedule, and decide on the road. Montenegro, the Albanian Alps and Kalymnos lead the list, and the researched options all sit south and east where the weather improves through late September. If the timing falls right, the Kalymnos climbing festival runs 2-4 October and would make a fitting finale; the flight home floats in the 3-8 October window either way.

The order comes from the calendar rather than preference. Alpine hiking peaks in early September and the huts and lifts start closing from about the 20th, while everything south of the Alps, from the Greek islands to the Balkan coast, improves as October approaches. So Italy goes first and the warm places wait for us to arrive unhurried. The candidates for the open weeks, with their selling points, are on Destinations.

Go to Decisions

Time-sensitive

Fares to Europe spiked earlier in 2026 and are settling as capacity returns, but September sits at the pricey end of the year, so sooner still beats later. Course dates everywhere are by enquiry, and September-October fills first. Alta Via rifugios are booking out now. The deadlines are ordered on Logistics.

What we said we wanted

The course comes first: the point is to fly home as lead climbers with a kit each, and any climbing after that is a bonus rather than an obligation. The first fortnight is planned and the rest is deliberately not; we would rather choose from a researched menu on the road than march through a schedule. Beyond that the trip leans outdoors: hiking, rock, sea swims and mountain food ahead of queueing for sights. We spend carefully by default and deliberately when something earns it, keep transit short relative to time on the ground, and want weather that's warm without being punishing.
