The recommended plan

Fly into Venice and hike the Dolomites while the Alps are having their best fortnight of the year: Tre Cime, Seceda, the Lagazuoi war tunnels, rifugio lunches, and a via ferrata or two as a warm-up for the rock. In week two, drop south to Arco on Lake Garda, the birthplace of European sport climbing, for a lead course with a UIAGM guide, then walk down a street with seventeen gear shops and buy the kit. Spend the last days leading easy classics above the lake. That is the complete 3-week trip. If we extend, fly to Kalymnos exactly as the island comes into season and climb on our own gear for one or two weeks, finishing, if we run the full five, at the international climbing festival on 2-4 October.

The shape
Dolomitesweeks 1-2 · hike + via ferrata
Arcoweeks 2-3 · course + gear + first leads
Kalymnosweeks 4-5 optional · climb + festival

Why this order: the season decides it. Alpine hiking peaks in early September and winds down from the 20th; the Greek islands only get better as October approaches. Mountains first, then follow the good weather south. The full reasoning, and every alternative we compared (Spain, Slovenia, Canada, South Africa, Turkey), lives on Destinations.

What this trip is optimising for

  • The course above all: come home lead climbers with our own kit. Extra climbing is the bonus, not the requirement.
  • Outdoors over tourism: hiking, rock, sea swims and mountain food; no queueing for generic sights.
  • Value with deliberate splurges: economic by default, and when we spend, we know why (the private course option, one long rifugio lunch, the celebration dinner in Arco).
  • Adventure over transit: two bases in the Dolomites, one town for the course, one island for the tail. Nothing under three nights.
  • Warm but not too hot: 20-25° climbing days in Arco, cool ridgelines in the Dolomites, and the island saved for when it stops being 30°.
