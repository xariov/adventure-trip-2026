The recommended plan

Fly into Venice and spend the first two weeks hiking the Dolomites, which are at their best in early September: Tre Cime, Seceda, the Lagazuoi war tunnels, long rifugio lunches, and a via ferrata or two to get comfortable on steep rock. In week two, drop south to Arco on Lake Garda, where European sport climbing more or less began. Do the lead course there with a UIAGM guide and buy a kit each from the town's seventeen gear shops, in whichever order we decide, then spend the last days leading easy classics above the lake. Home at three weeks with the thing we came for.

A fourth week is possible and doesn't have to involve a rope. Kalymnos comes into season right about then if we want to keep climbing; Sicily, Montenegro, Slovenia and a few others are there if we don't. A fifth week only makes sense if we point it at something specific, and this year there is something specific: the Kalymnos climbing festival and Arco's Rock Master both run 2-4 October.

The order comes from the calendar rather than preference. Alpine hiking peaks in early September and the huts and lifts start closing from about the 20th, while everything south of the Alps, from the Greek islands to Sicily to the Balkan coast, improves as October approaches. So the mountains go first and the warm places wait. The comparisons behind all of this, including the places that didn't make the cut, are on Destinations.

Go to Decisions

Time-sensitive

Fares to Europe spiked earlier in 2026 and are settling as capacity returns, but September sits at the pricey end of the year, so sooner still beats later. Course dates everywhere are by enquiry, and September-October fills first. Alta Via rifugios are booking out now. The deadlines are ordered on Logistics.

What we said we wanted

The course comes first: the point is to fly home as lead climbers with a kit each, and any climbing after that is a bonus rather than an obligation. Beyond that the trip leans outdoors: hiking, rock, sea swims and mountain food ahead of queueing for sights. We spend carefully by default and deliberately when something earns it, keep transit short relative to time on the ground (nothing under three nights in one place), and want weather that's warm without being punishing, which in September means the Alps early and the hot places late.
