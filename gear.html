Getting the price down in the shop

Quickdraw 6-packs run 25-30% cheaper per draw than singles, and rope-plus-rope-bag bundles are common. On a EUR 1,000+ single purchase at an independent Arco shop, 5-10% off or thrown-in extras (chalk, maillons, tape) is realistic; 15% is optimistic. Italian ticket prices sit near RRP, so a negotiated 10% may only match Bergfreunde's everyday online price. What the shop is really selling: everything in stock, shoes properly fitted, and advice calibrated to the crags we will stand under the next morning. That is worth the small margin.

Where to buy

Arco, Via Segantini (the plan)

  • Gobbi Sport, Via Segantini 72: full hardware range, trading since 1985.
  • Vertical Sport, Via Segantini 5: climbing and via ferrata specialist, La Sportiva dealer, open close to 360 days a year.
  • Oliunìd: Italy's big online climbing retailer, with two storefronts on Via Segantini.
  • SPORTLER Alpin, Via Segantini 112, and Red Point Mountain Equipment, Via Segantini 120.
  • Plus La Sportiva and Montura brand stores. Walk the street once for prices, buy on the second pass.

Backups and top-ups

  • SPORTLER Alpine Flagship, Bolzano: eight floors, the biggest mountain shop in South Tyrol; useful if we want anything while still in the Dolomites (but hauling a rope around the hiking leg makes no sense; wait for Arco).
  • Decathlon Trento: 35-40 minutes from Arco for cheap accessories (tape, approach snacks tubs, crag bag).
  • Kalymnos: Wild Sport and ClimbRock in Masouri plus Climber's Nest in Armeos. Full kit is buyable at mostly RRP; fine for top-ups, forgotten items and a guidebook, wrong place for the big purchase. Note: no rope or quickdraw rental on the island, which is another argument for owning the kit before arriving.

Flying home with it

  • No restrictions: ropes, carabiners, quickdraws, harnesses and helmets are not dangerous goods and are fine in checked baggage.
  • Chalk goes in the checked bag: powders over 350 g are restricted in carry-on on international flights out of Australia and similar rules apply elsewhere; checked is unrestricted.
  • Book the right fare tier, not excess baggage: the kit is only ~5 kg each on top of normal luggage. Emirates Saver, Qatar Convenience or Singapore Standard fares give 25-30 kg each (50-60 kg for the couple), which swallows it whole. Avoid the 20 kg basement fares; buying 5 kg of excess later costs AUD 100-230.
  • VAT refund: Italy's tax-free threshold is EUR 70 per store per day and the net refund is 10-14%, but goods must look effectively unused at EU exit; weeks-used gear can be refused. Treat it as a possible bonus, not part of the budget.

Not buying

  • Trad gear, cams, nuts: different discipline, different trip.
  • A second rope, alpine draws, multi-pitch kit: not until the sport basics are consolidated.
  • Via ferrata sets: rentable in the Dolomites for EUR 15-35 a day, used for two or three days total. Renting wins.
  • Anything before the course: the course changes what we would choose. Shoes are the only defensible early buy if an Australian sale appears before we fly.
