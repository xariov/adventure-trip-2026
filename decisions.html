Decisions

Work down the list in order; the early calls unlock the later ones, and each comes with the date it stops being free to put off. Decided items stay here for the record.

1. How long, and when? Decided.

Decided 19 July

Five weeks. Depart Brisbane 1 September, land Venice on the 2nd, fly home from Greece in the flexible 3-8 October window (the exact date follows the fares, and the Kalymnos festival on 2-4 October stays catchable inside it). Italy gets the first two weeks at most; the remaining three are decided on the road.

2. Where do we do the course? Decided: Arco.

Decided 19 July Book it now

Arco, in week two (around 12-16 September, exact days set with the guide). It wins on season, price, flexibility and the gear shops; the exact course names and the booking emails are on Lead course. Kalymnos instruction stays relevant only as a supervised first-leads day if we end up on the island.

3. Dolomites: valley bases or hut-to-hut?

Open Decide this month

  • a) Two valley bases + day hikes: Val Gardena then Cortina, every big-ticket hike from the list, total flexibility when a weather front rolls through, real beds and showers every night.
  • b) Add a 4-day Alta Via 1 section: Passo Falzarego to Passo Duran, the most scenic stretch and the easiest to book. Half board €55-80 pp/night, separate email to every hut, deposits often non-refundable, cash needed.

Take a, with b as an upgrade if the hut emails come back easy. The day-hike version loses almost none of the scenery and keeps flexibility in the one leg where weather matters most. If the hut evenings appeal, the emails go out this week or not at all.

4. Where does the hire car make sense?

Open Leg by leg, not whole-trip

A car is close to certain for parts of the trip; the question is which parts. The working split: none in Arco (crags and shops are walkable), optional in the Dolomites (the guest-pass buses genuinely cover the plan, but a car buys Val di Funes convenience and rainy-day range for ~US$25-45 a day, with the €40 Tre Cime toll and the 12 September Sellaronda road closure as the catches), close to essential in Montenegro (Kotor to Durmitor has no useful bus, ~€25-35 a day locally), and unnecessary on Kalymnos (scooters). The Balkans question is now answered: buses beat the car across the border. A Montenegro rental into Albania needs written authorisation, a Green Card and €75-95 in fees, one-way drops barely exist, and a car cannot follow the Valbona-Theth pass hike anyway, while the bus, ferry and furgon chain covers every leg for €8-27 each. So: hire inside Montenegro for the Kotor-Durmitor days, hand it back, and ride the chain from there.

5. How much of the three open weeks gets pre-booked?

Open Decide before leaving Australia

The philosophy is settled: no fixed itinerary for weeks three to five, choose on the road from the researched candidates (Montenegro, the Albanian Alps and Kalymnos lead; Slovenia, Sicily, Istria and the rest stay on the sheet). What still needs a call is how much scaffolding to put under that freedom:

  • a) Book nothing but the exit: hold a flexible or late-booked flight home in the 3-8 October window and book every bed on the road. Maximum freedom; the risk is Kalymnos in festival week, which fills well ahead.
  • b) Book the exit plus refundable Kalymnos rooms for 30 September - 5 October: keeps the festival finale genuinely available without committing to it (free cancellation studios exist on the island). Everything before the island stays loose.
  • c) Rough-book the corridor: refundable first nights in Kotor and Shkodra as well, so each hop has a landing pad. Less to arrange from a phone in a gorge.

b is the sensible floor: it protects the one thing in the open weeks that actually sells out. Whether to go to c depends on how much booking-from-the-road appeals once we are out there.

6. The festival weekend, 2-4 October

Open Decide on the road, protect it now

The trip ends inside the festival window, so this is no longer about extending; it is about whether the last weekend is the Kalymnos climbing festival (clinics, films, excursions, several thousand climbers, and we take part rather than watch) or a quiet finish wherever we happen to be, with the flight home floating to whichever early-October day prices best. Nothing forces the call early; what it needs is the refundable Kalymnos rooms from question 5, booked before the island fills. Rock Master in Arco shares the same dates, but with the tail heading south-east it would take a deliberate loop back to Italy to use it.

7. Gear: where and when do we buy?

Open Where and timing both open

One kit each (we both want our own gear), and harnesses and shoes are already owned, so this is about ropes, racks and hardware.

Where:

  • a) Both kits in Arco: 17 shops in 300 m, European prices, and a EUR 1,200+ two-kit basket to negotiate with.
  • b) At home before flying: pay Australian prices and haul ~15 kg both ways for no benefit.
  • c) Shoes topped up at home first: only if a genuine sale appears; otherwise Arco is La Sportiva's backyard and fits shoes properly.

When, if it is Arco:

  • a) Just before the course: learn on our own gear, guide checks the purchases, belay-device muscle memory lands on the device we own. Needs the provider's OK to run the course on our equipment; ask when booking.
  • b) Just after: try the instructor's gear first, buy with course-sharpened opinions.
  • c) Split: personal hardware before, ropes and racks whenever we feel ready.

On where, a. On when, the trade-offs are laid out on Gear and the call is ours; the current lean is before the course.
