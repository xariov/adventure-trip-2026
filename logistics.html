Long-haul flights

2026 flight reality check

Since the Middle East disruption that began in February 2026, Australia-Europe economy fares are running roughly 20-30% above normal and some Gulf-carrier frequencies are reduced. Specifically for us: Virgin Australia's Brisbane-Doha flights are suspended until 8 December 2026 and Qatar Airways' own Brisbane frequency is reduced, so verify any Qatar-coded itinerary actually operates before paying for it. Singapore Airlines and Cathay Pacific route around the region entirely.

Venice vs Milan: fares overlap almost completely; Milan's floor is maybe AUD 50-150 lower, but Venice is the better Dolomites gateway. Fly into Venice unless a Milan fare is dramatically cheaper.

The open-jaw question (into Venice, home from Athens, for the 4-5 week versions): fare construction usually adds only 0-10% (plan on AUD 100-300 pp). The catch is carrier choice: Singapore Airlines and Cathay do not serve Athens at all, so a single-ticket open-jaw effectively means Qatar or Emirates. The workaround if we prefer Cathay/Singapore: book the plain Italy return and add a cheap one-way Athens-Milan hop (Aegean or easyJet, EUR 70-130) at the end. Both shapes work; decide with the trip length.

The bang-for-buck read: each extension week costs 8-10% of the total for what is arguably the highest-payoff part of the trip, because the expensive fixed costs (flights, course, gear) are already sunk by week 3. On pure economics, longer is better here.

China Eastern routing knocks roughly AUD 2,400-2,800 off any scenario for the couple, in exchange for longer transits. A worthy discussion for the Decisions list.

Insurance that covers what we are actually doing

Most Australian policies exclude roped climbing or quietly require a licensed guide. As independent climbers after the course, the wording matters more than the price.

  • Freely (Cover-More's app brand), shortlisted: adventure add-on covers rock climbing "for bolted routes and using ropes and/or guides" up to 4,000 m. The "and/or" is the phrase that covers unguided sport climbing. Roughly AUD 400-700 base for the couple plus a few dollars per climbing day.
  • Cover-More adventure pack, shortlisted: covers unguided bolted climbing under 2,000 m; fine for Arco and Kalymnos, tight for high Dolomites via ferrata days, so check that leg specifically.
  • insure4less rock climbing plan: dedicated sport-climbing add-on to 3,500 m; quote-based.
  • World Nomads, caution: the activity list looks right, but the Australian help pages flag a professional-guide condition that would gut cover for independent climbing. Only with a current PDS check.
  • Fast Cover and SCTI: no - both require a licensed operator or instructor.
  • Worth adding regardless: Austrian Alpine Club membership (joinable by Australians, ~EUR 135 a year for a couple) includes mountain rescue to EUR 25,000 each, repatriation, and discounts at Alta Via rifugios. Cheap backstop; not a replacement for travel insurance (no cancellation or baggage cover, and the 24-hour emergency line must be called first).

Booking deadlines

This week
Settle trip length and course location (Decisions 1 and 2). If hut-to-hut appeals, the Alta Via rifugio emails go out now or the option lapses.
By end of July
Book long-haul flights (fares are elevated and not falling). Book or reserve the course: email the Arco guides with dates and the syllabus checklist, or deposit on the Kalymnos 13-18 Sept session before it fills. Buy insurance the same day as flights so cancellation cover starts immediately.
Early August
Book Val Gardena and Cortina-side rooms (early September is shoulder season but the good-value places go first) and the Arco apartment. If the festival finish is on: book the Kalymnos studio now; October is the island's peak month.
Mid August
Book the Italy-Kos flight if extending (Ryanair Bergamo pattern runs to 28 Sept, then check the 21 Oct pattern for the 5-week version). Reserve the Tre Cime toll slot or plan the Dobbiaco bus. Book any guided via ferrata day.
Before flying
Austrian Alpine Club membership, offline maps, rifugio cash float, and a folder with every booking. Leave a rope-sized hole in one duffel.
